Two major new features are now live in Case Compass: a complete **Referral Partner Management** system and **Waypoint**, our AI-powered intake scoring engine. Together, they transform how plaintiff law firms qualify cases, manage partner relationships, and convert prospects into signed clients.

## Referrals: Your Partner Network, Finally Organized

If your firm relies on co-counsel relationships, referral agreements, or partner networks to generate cases, you know how manual and fragmented that process can get. Emails, spreadsheets, verbal agreements — none of it is tracked in a way that scales.

**The Case Compass Referrals feature changes that.**

### What you can do now

- **Add referral partners** to your Case Compass account — individual attorneys, firms, or co-counsel relationships
- **Track every lead** a partner submits, from first contact through signed retainer
- **Set fee agreements** per partner — flat fees, percentage splits, or custom arrangements
- **Auto-generate fee records** the moment an intake is completed and a fee is triggered
- **Give partners their own portal** to submit leads and monitor status in real time — no back-and-forth required

### The partner portal

Referring attorneys get a clean, branded portal where they can:
- Submit new leads directly into your intake pipeline
- See the real-time status of every case they've referred
- View their fee records as they accumulate

No login to your internal Case Compass instance. No access to other cases. Just the information that's relevant to their partnership with your firm.

![Referral partner portal](/images/referrer-portal.png)

### Fee management built in

Define fee structures per partner when you onboard them. When a lead they submitted completes intake and is signed, Case Compass automatically creates a fee record at the agreed-upon rate. Monthly reporting is generated automatically — your billing team or accounting staff will thank you.

## Waypoint: AI Scoring That Tells You Which Cases to Take

Your intake team sees hundreds of leads. Some are strong cases. Some aren't. Right now, the difference between them often comes down to who answers the phone, how experienced they are, and how much time they have.

**Waypoint removes that variance.**

### How it works

Waypoint is a fully customizable AI scoring engine built directly into the Case Compass intake flow. The moment a claimant completes intake, Waypoint evaluates their submission against a scoring rubric you define — and surfaces a score before your team opens the file.

Every score comes with:
- A **numeric rating** (0–100)
- **Category-level breakdowns** — case value, liability, evidence quality, urgency, red flags
- **A written summary** explaining the score in plain language
- **Flags** for disqualifying factors or standout strengths

### Custom criteria per practice area

Waypoint isn't a one-size-fits-all tool. You define what a strong case looks like for your firm.

A mass tort firm running an IEEPA tariff refund campaign has completely different criteria than a workers' comp firm or a personal injury practice. Waypoint supports:
- Multiple criterion sets per practice area
- Weighted scoring by category
- Firm-specific red flags and must-have thresholds
- Drag-and-drop reordering of criteria priority

![Waypoint criteria management](/images/waypoint-criteria-list.png)

### Privacy-first AI: BYOK model

Waypoint uses AI to analyze intake submissions and generate scores — and we've built it with data privacy as a first principle.

**Waypoint uses your own API credentials (Bring Your Own Key).** When you configure Waypoint, you connect your own OpenAI API key. Your intake data is sent directly to OpenAI under your account — it never flows through Case Compass servers in a way that could expose it to a shared model or third-party training pipeline.

This means:
- Your claimant data never trains anyone else's model
- You maintain full control and audit rights
- There's no dependency on Case Compass's AI credentials or shared infrastructure

For firms handling sensitive personal injury, workers' comp, or mass tort data, this architecture is a meaningful differentiator.

### Getting started with Waypoint

1. Go to **Waypoint → Criteria** in your Case Compass dashboard
2. Create your first criterion set for your primary practice area
3. Add criteria using the natural language editor
4. Set weights and scoring priorities
5. Run a test score using an existing intake to calibrate

Your team will start seeing Waypoint scores on new intakes immediately.
